# Content quality rules for CodersSecret SEO

CodersSecret is a learning website. Content must serve learners first.

## People-first content

A page should provide clear value to developers and engineers. Strong pages include:

- simple explanation
- production context
- practical example
- trade-offs
- mistakes to avoid
- checklist or steps
- related learning links

## Good article structure

Use this structure when suitable:

1. Clear H1.
2. Short summary/dek.
3. What the topic is.
4. Why it matters in production.
5. How it works.
6. Diagram/example/code.
7. Security, reliability, or architecture trade-offs if relevant.
8. Common mistakes.
9. Practical checklist.
10. Related CodersSecret content.
11. Sources/spec links when needed.

## Human writing style

Use:

- short clear sentences
- plain technical language
- concrete examples
- exact terms
- active voice
- useful headings

Avoid:

- "unlock the power"
- "dive deep"
- "game-changing"
- "revolutionize"
- "in today's fast-paced digital world"
- "ultimate guide" unless truly comprehensive
- "comprehensive" when the page is not comprehensive
- generic filler intros
- fake enthusiasm
- inflated claims
- word count padding

## SEO keywords

- Use the natural terms engineers search for.
- Put important terms in title, H1, first paragraph, headings, and anchors when natural.
- Do not repeat keywords unnaturally.
- Do not create many near-duplicate pages for keyword variants.
- Do not write for "AEO/GEO" hacks; Google says foundational SEO and unique valuable content still matter for AI search.

## Accuracy

- Do not invent claims.
- Do not invent dates or update timestamps.
- Do not invent examples that imply real incidents unless labeled hypothetical.
- Use official docs for security, standards, cloud, Kubernetes, framework, and database specifics when possible.
- Mention limitations and trade-offs.
- For security content, avoid unsafe operational guidance that could help abuse.

## AI-assisted content

Allowed only when:

- it adds structure to original content or helps draft useful explanations
- a human-quality review has corrected errors
- content is original and helpful
- content is not mass-generated to manipulate rankings
- content is not thin, generic, or duplicative

Disallowed:

- scaled pages with no additional value
- paraphrased docs without original explanation
- generic articles only targeting keywords
- fake author/date/source/comments
- content written only to manipulate Google or AI responses
